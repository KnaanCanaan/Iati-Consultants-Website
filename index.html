Iati Consultants

Iati Consultants delivers quality reports, seminars and workshops addressing geopolitical, development and governance challenges in the Pacific. Our team employs catered methodologies to examine issues ranging from how to navigate the Pacific’s strategic and security environment, governance institutions and systems, and sustainable development problems.

We actively assist our clients in understanding emerging trends, anticipated risks, and opportunities in this region and beyond. Our work informs strategic thinking by interpreting regional complexities and formulating clear and effective policy options. We empower informed decision-making for the fast-changing dynamics of Pacific geopolitics, governance and development.

Our Purpose

Iati Consultants’ foundational purpose is to deliver expert geopolitical, governance and development consultation services for governmental agencies, inter-governmental agencies and businesses operating in the Pacific. We are dedicated to enhancing our clients’ understanding of this dynamic area through rigorous analysis or primary and secondary research, empowering them to navigate complex political landscapes with confidence and precision.

Our approach is defined by a bespoke suite of services, each meticulously tailored to address distinct topical requirements and challenges, and client’s requirements. By forging close, collaborative partnerships with key institutions and communities across the Pacific, we ensure our work is contextually grounded and actionable. This deep integration enables us to work in concert with you, transforming nuanced research into the successful achievement of your specific strategic objectives while benefitting local communities, non-government organizations, and governments..

Founder Biography

Dr. Iati has over 16 years of distinguished expertise in Pacific geopolitics and international relations, combining academic teaching, research and publications with consultancy work.

He was a senior lecturer at Otago University before taking up his current role as Senior Lecturer at the Politics and International Relations Programme at Victoria University of Wellington, and Pacific Security Fellow at the Centre for Strategic Studies.

He has co-authored and edited publications such as "New Zealand and the World", "The China Alternative" and "China in Oceania". His work approach consists of analysing current events through a methodical and unbiased perspective.
