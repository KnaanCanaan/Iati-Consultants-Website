Iati Consultants

IATI CONSULTANTS does policy research, analysis, and reports. We deliver quality reports, seminars and workshops addressing geopolitical, development and governance challenges in the Pacific. Our team employs catered methodologies to examine issues ranging from how to navigate the Pacific’s strategic and security environment, governance institutions and systems, and sustainable development problems.

We actively assist our clients in understanding emerging trends, anticipated risks, and opportunities in this region and beyond. Our work informs strategic thinking by interpreting regional complexities and formulating clear and effective policy options. We empower informed decision-making for the fast- changing dynamics of Pacific geopolitics, governance and development.

Our Purpose

IATI CONSULTANTS’ key goal is to create win-win engagement and relationships between clients and Pacific communities and governments. We deliver expert services that cater for government agencies, international organizations, civil society, and businesses interested in Pacific geopolitics, governance and development issues. We have a bespoke suite of services, and each will be tailored to address different requirements.

Having over 20 years research experience in the Pacific, our team has forged close and collaborative partnerships with key institutions, communities, and peoples across the region. Combining research expertise with rigorous and balanced analysis, we help clients achieve their objectives while benefitting local communities, non-government organizations, and governments.

Founder Biography

Dr. Iati has over 16 years of distinguished expertise in Pacific geopolitics and international relations, combining academic teaching, research and publications with consultancy work.

He was a senior lecturer at Otago University before taking up his current role as Senior Lecturer at the Politics and International Relations Programme at Victoria University of Wellington, and Pacific Security Fellow at the Centre for Strategic Studies.

He has co-authored and edited publications such as "New Zealand and the World", "The China Alternative" and "China in Oceania". His work approach consists of analysing current events through a methodical and unbiased perspective.
